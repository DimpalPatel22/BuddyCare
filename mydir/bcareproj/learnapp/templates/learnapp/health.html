Common Health Concerns & Prevention

Understanding common diseases and health issues is the first step to keeping your pets safe and happy. BuddyCare is here to guide you in spotting early signs and preventing serious problems.

Dogs

  • Fleas & Ticks: Check fur regularly, use vet-approved preventatives.

  • Parvovirus: Ensure vaccinations are up to date; watch for vomiting or diarrhea.

  • Obesity: Feed balanced meals and provide daily exercise.

  • Dental Issues: Brush teeth regularly and provide chew toys.

Cats

  • Feline Upper Respiratory Infection: Keep vaccinations current; maintain hygiene.

  • Fleas: Check fur and use safe preventatives.

  • Urinary Issues: Provide clean water; monitor litter box habits.

  • Obesity: Control portions and encourage play.

Birds

  • Feather Plucking: Ensure mental stimulation and
    proper social interaction; provide toys and perches.

  • Beak Deformities: Provide appropriate chew toys and cuttlebone;
    ensure a balanced diet rich in nutrients.

  • Eye Infections: Maintain cage hygiene;
    avoid exposure to drafts and dusty environments.

  • Bumblefoot: Keep perches soft and clean; avoid hard surfaces.

Pet First Aid & Emergency Tips üêæ

Quick actions can make all the difference! Here are some simple steps you can follow to keep your pets safe in common emergencies.

Stop Bleeding

Apply a clean cloth or bandage to the wound. Keep your pet calm and still.

Choking

Check the airway carefully. If something is stuck, try gentle removal or contact your vet immediately.

Heatstroke

Move your pet to a shaded area, offer cool water, and wet their body. Contact a vet if symptoms persist.

Poisoning

Do not induce vomiting. Call your vet immediately and provide details of what your pet ingested.

Home Remedies for Minor Pet & Street Animal Issues üè°

For minor sicknesses or discomfort, these simple home remedies can help your pets and injured street animals recover safely. Always monitor them closely and contact a vet if symptoms persist or worsen.

Dogs

  • Upset Stomach: Offer boiled rice and chicken; keep water available.
  • Mild Cold: Keep warm, give steam exposure for 10 min, maintain hydration.
  • Minor Cuts: Clean with boiled water, apply antiseptic cream.

Cats

  • Hairball: Offer small amount of olive oil or commercial hairball paste.
  • Mild Cold: Keep warm, maintain clean bedding, steam exposure if possible.
  • Minor Cuts: Clean with boiled water, keep area dry and monitor.

Birds

  • Mild Weakness: Offer sugar water or soaked seeds to boost energy.
  • Feather Plucking Stress: Provide quiet environment, offer toys or perches.
  • Minor Injuries: Clean gently with boiled water; avoid harsh handling.

Injured Street Animals

  • Minor Cuts or Scrapes: Clean with boiled water, apply mild antiseptic if available.
  • Dehydration: Offer clean water gradually.
  • Exhaustion: Provide a safe, warm, quiet shelter for rest.
