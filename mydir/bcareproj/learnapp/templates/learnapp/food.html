Do's & Don'ts for Pet Nutrition

Do ‚úÖ

Hydration

  • Feed fresh water daily.
  • Check water bowls are clean and filled.

Balanced Diet

  • Include proteins, fats, and carbs in proper ratio.
  • Offer seasonal fruits and vegetables.

Hygiene

  • Use clean bowls and utensils.
  • Keep feeding area sanitary.

Regular Monitoring

  • Check pet‚Äôs body weight and condition regularly.
  • Observe eating habits and adjust diet if necessary.

Don't ‚ùå

Unsafe Foods

  • Avoid chocolate, caffeine, alcohol.
  • No spoiled or moldy food.

Overfeeding

  • Do not overfeed or free-feed constantly.
  • Avoid excessive human food.

Dangerous Items

  • Never give bones that can splinter.
  • Follow vet-recommended supplements only.

Neglect

  • Do not ignore signs of illness or poor appetite.
  • Never leave pets without supervision for long periods during feeding.

Start with small, frequent meals; soft, easy-to-digest food; monitor for stress or illness.

Adopted Pet

Follow vet guidance for diet, introduce new food gradually, and ensure clean water is available at all times.

Quick Recipes & Tips

Easy homemade food ideas for pets, street animals, and birds.

Rice + Boiled Chicken

Healthy meal for dogs. Boil chicken, mix with rice, and serve warm.

Curd Rice

Great for summer. Keeps dogs hydrated and cool.

Soaked Grains

Perfect for pigeons and birds. Soak grains overnight for easy digestion.

Roti + Milk Alternatives

Ideal for street dogs. Avoid spicy curries or oily foods.
