Dangerous Animal Help – Stay Safe, Save Lives

Guidelines to handle aggressive or dangerous animals calmly — at home, office, or outdoors.

⚠ Common Dangerous Situations

🐕‍🦺

Aggressive Dogs

Stay calm, avoid direct eye contact, and back away slowly. Do not run or shout.

🐍

Snake Encounters

Maintain distance, do not attempt to kill. Call a snake rescuer immediately.

🐒

Monkey Trouble

Do not make eye contact or offer food. Secure doors and windows calmly.

🐂

Aggressive Cattle

Keep safe distance, avoid provoking, and alert local authorities if needed.

🦅

Birds of Prey

If trapped inside, switch off fans and open windows. Let them fly out naturally.

🐀

Rats & Rodents

Common in cities during monsoon and garbage areas. Avoid direct contact, never corner them, and keep surroundings clean.

When They Enter Your Living Space

Sometimes animals or birds accidentally enter our homes. Stay calm and follow these steps to ensure safety for both you and them.

Stay Calm & Isolate the Area

Close doors to other rooms, keep children and pets away, and create a quiet space.

Give an Exit Path

Open windows or balconies to let them leave naturally without stress.

Turn Off Fans & Bright Lights

Helps birds or small creatures calm down and avoid injury.

Call Local Rescue

If it’s a snake, monkey, or dangerous animal, immediately contact rescuers or authorities.

🔎 Examples of Common Situations

If an Eagle Enters

Turn off ceiling fans, dim the lights, and open all windows or balconies. Stay away and let it fly out naturally.

If a Snake Enters

Do not try to handle or kill it. Keep a safe distance, close the room, and immediately call a certified snake rescuer.

If a Monkey Enters

Do not make eye contact, avoid offering food, and calmly secure all exits. Call forest officials if the monkey doesn’t leave.

If a Stray Dog Enters

Stay calm, open the exit door, and gently guide them out without shouting. also you can give them some living place If aggressive, wait for help from local rescuers.

Quick Do’s & Don’ts

✅ Do’s

  • Observe from a safe distance before taking action.
  • Keep emergency numbers handy.
  • Ensure pets are in a separate safe area.
  • Document the situation (photo/video) for rescuers if safe.

❌ Don’ts

  • Do not attempt DIY traps for dangerous animals.
  • Do not panic or create noise that may agitate them.
  • Do not block exits — always let them leave freely.
  • Do not leave food or waste nearby that attracts them.
